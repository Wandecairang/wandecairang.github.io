About

My name is Wande Cairang (Tsering), and I am originally from Tibet. I am a Ph.D. candidate in Nuclear Science & Engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), working in the Mesoscale Nuclear Materials Lab under the supervision of Prof. Michael P. Short. My research focuses on the coupled effects of irradiation and corrosion in structural materials exposed to liquid metals and molten salts, including stainless steels, Fe–Ni alloys, refractory metals, and SiC-based ceramics. My skills include ion irradiation, characterizations such as SEM, TEM, FIB, and basic DFT simulations.

Research Experience

Graduate Research Assistant, MIT Mesoscale Nuclear Materials Lab (2021 – Present)

  • Simultaneous proton irradiation and dissolution corrosion of SS316L in liquid metals (Pb or Pb-Bi alloys).
  • Dealloying of Fe–Ni alloys and the influence of proton irradiation on dealloying patterns.
  • Synergistic effects of irradiation and corrosion on sintered SiC with different additives in molten salts and liquid metals.

Graduate Research Assistant, Xi’an Jiaotong University (2018 – 2021)

  • Synergy of ionizing irradiation and oxidation of molybdenum.
  • Oxidation behavior of Mo exposed to oxygen-saturated liquid lead–bismuth eutectic.
  • Enhancement of corrosion resistance of Mo by doping rare-earth oxides such as La2O3.
